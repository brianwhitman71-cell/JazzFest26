The Sacred Laws of Jazz Fest

Read these. Memorize them. Then go buy a beer inside like you were always going to anyway.

✓

Yes, Bring This

  • Soft-sided coolers up to 14"×14"×14" — aka your "snack suitcase," filled with waters you'll abandon by noon
  • Factory-sealed non-alcoholic drinks — security's way of saying "nice try, we smelled you"
  • Food in clear bags — the transparency is for them, the crawfish bread is for you
  • Folding chairs under 33" tall — for when you've had four Abitas and the ground starts to sway
  • Blankets & small rugs — claim your turf early, defend it with your life
  • Small umbrellas & parasols — it will be 94° and also somehow raining, simultaneously
  • Cameras with lenses under 4" — document the chaos, not the carnage
  • GoPros — for the footage you'll never edit but will reference for the rest of your life
  • Binoculars — for spying on the crowd from a distance or confirming yes, that IS your friend passed out by the Blues Tent
  • Fanny packs — the most practical, least embarrassing item you own right now
  • Sunscreen & bug spray — New Orleans sun + New Orleans mosquitoes = a humbling experience
  • Hats, bandanas, rain ponchos — the full outdoor degenerate starter kit
  • Prescription meds in original bottles — yes, Advil counts, yes you will need it
  • Earplugs — ironic to bring to a music festival but genuinely wise after hour six
  • Cash — the beer vendors accept it, God accepts it, and that's enough
✕

Leave It Home, Champ

  • Outside alcohol — they know. They always know. And honestly, the Abita stand is 40 feet away
  • Glass containers — one dropped bottle near the Blues Tent and you're a legend for all the wrong reasons
  • Hard-sided coolers — this is a festival, not a tailgate at your brother-in-law's house
  • Professional cameras with lenses ≥ 4" — unless you have a media pass, put the paparazzi energy away
  • Video cameras & recording gear — your shaky iPhone footage is already more than enough
  • Drones — nobody wants to see a drone at Jazz Fest. We all agreed on this
  • Weapons of any kind — you are here to hear Trombone Shorty, not to start beef
  • Fireworks — the music is the fireworks, calm down
  • Laser pointers — banned everywhere forever, please stop
  • Large beach umbrellas — you are not at the beach, you are at Jazz Fest, and you are blocking everyone
  • Chairs taller than 33" — a throne is not appropriate here unless you are Irma Thomas
  • Pets — your dog does not want to be here. Your dog wants to be home on the couch
  • Backpacks over 14"×14"×6" — if it could fit a small child, it's too big
  • Large strollers — you brought a baby to Jazz Fest and that's admirable, but the stroller stays small
  • Re-entry — once you leave, you're out. Choose wisely. The crawfish Monica will still be there
ⓘ All bags get searched at the gate — smile, be cool, don't be weird about it. Rules can change year to year so double-check at nojazzfest.com before you show up with something you're going to have to explain.
